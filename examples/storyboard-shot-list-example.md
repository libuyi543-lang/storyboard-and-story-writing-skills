# Example Storyboard Shot List

| Shot | Beat | Purpose | Viewpoint | Framing/Size | Height/Angle | Composition/Focus | Blocking/Movement | Cut Logic |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Arrival | Establish who is in the room and what emotional distance exists | Objective | Wide two-shot | Eye level | Character A foreground left, Character B deeper right, empty space between them | B enters and stops, A stays seated | Start with readable geography |
| 2 | First pressure | Show B bringing the problem into A's space | A-leaning | Medium on B | Slightly low | Focus on document in B's hand, then B's face | B steps forward and places paper down | Cut for new story information |
| 3 | Refusal | Show A's emotional resistance | A-attached | Medium close-up on A | Eye level | Paper blurred in foreground, A as visual focus | A looks at paper but avoids eye contact | Reaction changes meaning of previous action |
| 4 | Escalation | Reframe both characters inside growing conflict | Objective | Tight two-shot | Slightly high | Faces now closer in frame, space compressed | B leans in, A stands | Cut because power balance changes |
