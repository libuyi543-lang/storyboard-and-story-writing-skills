# Revision Checklist

Use this when a draft feels boring, unclear, preachy, scattered, or emotionally thin.

## A. Core Hook

- Can the story be summarized in one compelling sentence?
- Does the opening activate curiosity, concern, or attachment quickly enough?
- Is the audience clearly told what to care about?

## B. Central Question

- Can you state the main question as `Can the protagonist ___?`
- Does the story also carry emotional and value-level stakes?
- If only the event layer exists, deepen the draft before polishing.

## C. Protagonist

- Is the protagonist active enough?
- Is the goal or problem clear enough?
- Is there a contradiction, wound, or belief under pressure?
- Does the audience have enough reason to want to follow this person?

## D. Structure

- Is there a catalyst that breaks the old balance?
- Does the middle escalate instead of merely continuing?
- Is there a genuine reversal?
- Does the climax pay off all the right questions?
- Does the ending provide release?

## E. Scene Quality

- Does each scene have a purpose?
- Does each scene turn?
- Does each scene create the next expectation?
- Are any scenes only delivering information with no dramatic shift?

## F. Dialogue

- Is dialogue serving the scene objective?
- Are weak lines actually symptoms of weak scene construction?
- Are characters speaking from different wants and pressures?
- Is any exposition better shown through conflict, action, or omission?

## G. Multi-Line Stories

- Does each line have a distinct dramatic function?
- Do the lines cross or echo in meaningful ways?
- Could one line be removed without much loss? If yes, strengthen or cut it.

## H. Final Pass

When unsure what to revise first, use this order:

1. audience concern
2. protagonist goal/problem
3. central question layers
4. structure and reversal
5. scene turns
6. climax payoff
7. dialogue polish
