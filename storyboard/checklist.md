# Review Checklist

Use this checklist when revising an existing storyboard, shot list, animatic plan, or scene breakdown.

## A. Story Function

- Can the scene's purpose be stated in one sentence?
- Does every beat change information, emotion, power, tactic, or space?
- If a shot disappeared, would the story become less clear? If not, cut it.

## B. Viewpoint

- Is the audience relationship clear: objective, attached, or shifting?
- If attached, do look-shots and reactions actually support that attachment?
- If shifting, is the shift motivated and visible?

## C. Geography

- Can a reader reconstruct where everyone is?
- Is the axis stable enough to follow?
- If the axis breaks, is there a bridge through movement, eyeline, neutral framing, or viewpoint?

## D. Shot Size and Cut Pattern

- Are there same-size same-composition cuts that feel like accidental repeats?
- Is there drawer-pulling: wide-tight-wide-tight without purpose?
- Is the sequence gradually tightening or loosening where needed?

## E. Composition and Focus

- Does each frame have a clear first focus?
- Is composition carrying meaning or only decoration?
- Does the frame express the intended mood: pressure, distance, intimacy, isolation, chaos, calm?

## F. Movement and Blocking

- Does every move reveal, redirect, escalate, or transition?
- Are character movements helping the audience follow the scene?
- Is movement continuity helping cuts feel smoother?

## G. Rhythm and Paragraphing

- Can you identify the visual paragraphs of the scene?
- Does the rhythm change when tactics or emotion change?
- Are any shots too long after they stop delivering new information?

## H. Bias Toward Simplicity

- Can the same story be told with fewer setups?
- Can one stronger main shot replace two weak coverage shots?
- Is there any flourish that weakens clarity?

## Fast Repair Order

When a board feels bad, fix in this order:

1. scene purpose
2. beat structure
3. viewpoint
4. geography and axis
5. main shots
6. shot size sequence
7. composition and rhythm
8. extras and polish
